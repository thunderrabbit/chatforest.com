The enterprise AI market just flipped. Anthropic now captures [40% of enterprise LLM API spending](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/), up from 12% in 2023. OpenAI has [dropped to 27%](https://www.techmeme.com/251209/p35), down from 50% over the same period. The company that started as a safety-focused research lab has overtaken the company that kicked off the generative AI era — and the shift is accelerating.

## The Market Share Numbers

The [Menlo Ventures 2025 State of Generative AI report](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/), based on a [survey of 150 technical leaders](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/12/09/3202258/0/en/Menlo-Ventures-2025-State-of-Generative-AI-Report-Enterprise-Investment-Hit-37B-in-2025-Tripling-in-One-Year.html) across AI startups and large enterprises, shows the shift in production workloads:

| Provider | 2023 Share | 2025 Share | Direction |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Anthropic** | 12% | 40% | +28 points |
| **OpenAI** | 50% | 27% | -23 points |
| **Google** | ~15% | 20% | +5 points |
| **Others** | ~23% | 13% | -10 points |

Total enterprise LLM spending [more than doubled in six months](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/07/31/3125037/0/en/Enterprise-LLM-Spend-Reaches-8-4B-as-Anthropic-Overtakes-OpenAI-According-to-New-Menlo-Ventures-Report-on-LLM-Market.html) — from $3.5 billion in late 2024 to $8.4 billion by mid-2025, [then hit $37 billion for all of 2025](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/12/09/3202258/0/en/Menlo-Ventures-2025-State-of-Generative-AI-Report-Enterprise-Investment-Hit-37B-in-2025-Tripling-in-One-Year.html). This is not a zero-sum game. OpenAI's revenue is still growing. But Anthropic is growing faster, and the gap in market share has reversed completely.

The most telling data point comes from [Ramp](https://ramp.com/velocity/ai-index-march-2026), the corporate card and spend management platform that tracks actual enterprise purchasing in real time. As of March 2026, Anthropic captures [73% of all spending among companies making new AI purchasing decisions](https://sherwood.news/tech/anthropic-capturing-73-of-first-time-enterprise-ai-spend-up-from-50-in/) — up from a [50/50 split with OpenAI just ten weeks earlier](https://ramp.com/velocity/ai-index-february-2026).

Important context: about [79% of Anthropic's customers also pay for OpenAI](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/11/openai-anthropic-chatgpt-claude-subscriptions). Most enterprises are not replacing OpenAI — they're adding Anthropic as a second provider and then shifting the majority of new workloads there. The [marginal buyer is going 70%+ Anthropic](https://www.saastr.com/openai-vs-anthropic-ramp-data-shows-36-vs-12-penetration-but-velocity-curves-tell-a-different-story/).

## What's Driving the Shift

### API and Coding Dominance

The vast majority of enterprise LLM spend is not on chatbot subscriptions — it's [API usage by engineering teams](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/) building AI into products and using AI for coding. Claude Code [hit $2.5 billion in annualized revenue](https://byteiota.com/claude-code-hits-41-share-overtakes-copilots-38/) by February 2026, with [enterprise use accounting for over half](https://www.gradually.ai/en/claude-code-statistics/) of all Claude Code revenue. [Business subscriptions quadrupled](https://byteiota.com/claude-code-hits-41-share-overtakes-copilots-38/) since the start of 2026.

[Over 1,000 businesses now spend more than $1 million per year](https://www.the-ai-corner.com/p/anthropic-30b-arr-passed-openai-revenue-2026) on Anthropic services on an annualized basis, [up from roughly 500 just two months earlier](https://www.trendingtopics.eu/anthropic-overtakes-openai-in-revenue-hitting-30-billion-run-rate/). [Enterprise customers represent approximately 80%](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/) of Anthropic's revenue.

### Benchmark Dominance

Claude Opus 4.6 became the [first AI model to hold the #1 position across all three LMSYS Chatbot Arena leaderboards simultaneously](https://aiworld.eu/story/claude-opus-46-leader-of-the-leaderboards) — text, code, and search. No model from OpenAI, Google, or xAI has achieved this.

The specific numbers [as of April 6, 2026](https://aidevdayindia.org/blogs/lmsys-chatbot-arena-current-rankings/lmsys-chatbot-arena-high-elo-rankings.html):

| Leaderboard | #1 Model | Elo Score |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Text** | Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking | [1502](https://aidevdayindia.org/blogs/lmsys-chatbot-arena-current-rankings/lmsys-chatbot-arena-leaderboard-current-top-models.html) |
| **Code** | Claude Opus 4.6 | [1548](https://aidevdayindia.org/blogs/lmsys-chatbot-arena-current-rankings/lmsys-chatbot-arena-coding-leaderboard-2026.html) |
| **Search** | Claude Opus 4.6 | [#1](https://arena.ai/leaderboard) |

On the coding leaderboard, Anthropic models [occupy four of the top five positions](https://aidevdayindia.org/blogs/lmsys-chatbot-arena-current-rankings/lmsys-chatbot-arena-coding-leaderboard-2026.html). This matters because the [LMSYS Chatbot Arena uses blind head-to-head human preference evaluations](https://arena.ai/leaderboard) — it's the benchmark most resistant to gaming.

### Enterprise Distribution

Anthropic has built genuine enterprise distribution without relying on a platform partner. [More than 70% of Fortune 100 companies](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-raises-30-billion-series-g-funding-380-billion-post-money-valuation) now use Claude products. The company [shipped MCP connectors](https://winbuzzer.com/2026/02/25/anthropic-claude-cowork-13-enterprise-plugins-google-workspace-docusign-xcxwbn/) for [Google Drive, Google Calendar, Gmail, DocuSign](https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/anthropic-says-claude-code-transformed-programming-now-claude-cowork-is), Apollo, Clay, Outreach, SimilarWeb, MSCI, LegalZoom, FactSet, WordPress, and Harvey — extending Claude into the [enterprise software ecosystem](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/24/anthropic-claude-cowork-office-worker.html) directly.

Meanwhile, Claude Code [reached 41% of the professional developer market](https://byteiota.com/claude-code-hits-41-share-overtakes-copilots-38/) by February 2026, overtaking GitHub Copilot's 38% despite Copilot having a three-year head start and Microsoft's enterprise distribution behind it.

## The Revenue Crossover

Anthropic's annualized revenue has [reached $30 billion](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/anthropic-says-topped-30-billion-annualized-revenue), [surpassing OpenAI for the first time](https://winbuzzer.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-tops-30b-annualized-revenue-surpassing-openai-xcxwbn/). The growth rates tell the story:

| Metric | Anthropic | OpenAI |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Annualized revenue** | [~$30B](https://crypto.news/anthropic-revenue-just-hit-a-30-billion-run-rate/) | ~$25B |
| **Year-over-year growth** | [~10x](https://www.the-ai-corner.com/p/anthropic-30b-arr-passed-openai-revenue-2026) | ~3.4x |
| **Revenue trajectory** | Accelerating | Slowing |
| **Profitability** | Not profitable | [Not profitable](https://www.fastcompany.com/91522156/openai-doesnt-expect-to-be-profitable-until-at-least-2030-as-ai-costs-surge) |

Neither company is profitable. Anthropic's compute costs are enormous and growing. But the revenue crossover changes the competitive narrative and the IPO calculus.

OpenAI's revenue growth has [slowed from its earlier trajectory](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/18/ai-enterprise-revenue-anthropic-openai), which complicates its IPO narrative. The company closed a [$122 billion funding round at an $852 billion valuation](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/31/openai-funding-round-ipo.html) in March 2026 — but the revenue momentum that justified that valuation now belongs to its competitor.

Both companies are targeting late-2026 IPOs in what could be the [largest tech offerings in history](https://fortune.com/2026/03/20/spacex-openai-anthropic-could-be-3-of-the-biggest-venture-backed-ipos-of-all-time/):

| | Anthropic | OpenAI |
|--|-----------|--------|

| **Valuation** | [$380 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/12/anthropic-closes-30-billion-funding-round-at-380-billion-valuation.html) | [$852 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/31/openai-funding-round-ipo.html) |

| **Key backers** | Google, Lightspeed, Menlo | Amazon $50B, Nvidia $30B, SoftBank $30B |

OpenAI reportedly wants to list before Anthropic, with [CEO Sam Altman pushing for a Q4 2026 listing](https://www.pymnts.com/news/ipo/2026/openai-leaders-at-odds-over-ipo-plans/). However, [CFO Sarah Friar has reportedly told colleagues the company isn't ready](https://www.notebookcheck.net/OpenAI-CFO-reportedly-questions-Altman-s-600-billion-spending-and-2026-IPO-timeline.1267088.0.html), questioning the timeline and [voicing concerns about $600 billion in spending commitments](https://www.outlookbusiness.com/corporate/openai-ipo-tensions-cfo-sarah-friar-warns-against-sam-altmans-push-for-2026-listing). Anthropic now has the stronger growth story: higher revenue growth rate, market share leadership, and benchmark dominance.

The valuation gap is notable. OpenAI at $852 billion is 2.2x Anthropic's $380 billion — but Anthropic's revenue is now higher. If the IPO market prices on revenue multiples and growth rates, Anthropic's valuation could close that gap quickly.

[Fortune reported in April 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/04/07/spacex-openai-anthropic-reopen-ipo-market-crunchbase/) that SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic could together "reopen the IPO market — or drain it." These three offerings, if they all proceed, would represent an [unprecedented concentration of capital raises](https://www.marketplace.org/story/2026/04/08/why-2026-is-the-year-of-the-megaipo) in a single quarter.

## What OpenAI Still Has

This is not a story of OpenAI collapsing. The company retains significant advantages:

**Consumer dominance.** ChatGPT remains the most recognized AI brand globally. The super app consolidating chat, coding, search, and agent capabilities into a unified experience gives it consumer distribution that Anthropic hasn't matched.

**GPT-5.4's computer use.** GPT-5.4 is the [first general-purpose model with native computer-use capabilities](https://aihaven.com/news/openai-gpt-5-4-launch-computer-agents/), scoring [75% on OSWorld](https://www.nxcode.io/resources/news/gpt-5-4-complete-guide-features-pricing-models-2026) — surpassing the [human expert baseline of 72.4%](https://nerdleveltech.com/gpt-5-4-beats-humans-computer-use-ai-agents). No other model has crossed that threshold.

**Platform breadth.** OpenAI has Codex, DALL-E, Sora, Whisper, and an increasingly complete product suite. Anthropic is essentially a one-product company (Claude) with Claude Code as its breakout hit.

**Microsoft partnership.** The Azure integration gives OpenAI distribution in enterprise environments where IT procurement prefers to buy through existing cloud contracts. OpenAI still [holds 36% penetration among Ramp customers vs. Anthropic's 12%](https://www.saastr.com/openai-vs-anthropic-ramp-data-shows-36-vs-12-penetration-but-velocity-curves-tell-a-different-story/) — it's the new spending that's shifted, not the installed base.

**Ecosystem scale.** The [$297 billion in Q1 2026 startup funding](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/01/startup-funding-shatters-all-records-in-q1/) — driven by mega-rounds including OpenAI's own — the [Agentic AI Foundation](https://openai.com/index/agentic-ai-foundation/) co-founding, and [13+ acquisitions](https://tracxn.com/d/acquisitions/acquisitions-by-openai/__kElhSG7uVGeFk1i71Co9-nwFtmtyMVT7f-YHMn4TFBg) give it broader ecosystem reach.

## What Google Is Doing

Google is the quiet third player worth watching. Its enterprise LLM market share [grew from roughly 15% to 20%](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/) — not the dramatic swing that Anthropic achieved, but steady growth.

[Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-1-flash-lite/) launched at [$0.25 per million input tokens](https://cloud.google.com/vertex-ai/generative-ai/pricing) — aggressive pricing designed to undercut both Anthropic and OpenAI on cost-sensitive enterprise workloads. Google's strategy appears to be winning on price and infrastructure integration (Vertex AI, BigQuery, Cloud Run) rather than competing at the frontier model tier. Google's [seventh-generation TPU (Ironwood)](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4517292-google-to-launch-7th-gen-tpu-ironwood-in-coming-weeks-stepping-up-challenge-to-nvidia) gives it a [structural cost advantage](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/07/googles-decade-long-bet-on-tpus-companys-secret-weapon-in-ai-race.html) that neither Anthropic nor OpenAI can match with rented GPUs — part of the broader [custom AI chip race](/guides/custom-ai-chip-race-2026/) reshaping AI economics.

The [Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/) with [50+ partners](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/agent2agent-protocol-is-getting-an-upgrade) represents Google's bet that the protocol layer, not the model layer, is where value will accrue. It's a different competitive strategy than Anthropic's model-quality approach or OpenAI's platform breadth.

## What We Don't Know

**How sticky is the shift?** Anthropic is winning new deals, but [79% of its customers also pay OpenAI](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/11/openai-anthropic-chatgpt-claude-subscriptions). The multi-vendor pattern could reverse if OpenAI ships a model that retakes the Arena leaderboard.

**Revenue quality.** Neither company discloses gross margins. API revenue at scale could be higher-margin than consumer subscriptions, or it could be lower if enterprise customers negotiate volume discounts. The [$30 billion ARR figure](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/anthropic-says-topped-30-billion-annualized-revenue) doesn't tell us about profitability trajectory.

**Survey methodology limits.** The Menlo Ventures data comes from [150 technical leaders](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/). The [Ramp data](https://ramp.com/velocity/ai-index-march-2026) captures actual spending but only among Ramp's customer base (which [skews toward startups and growth-stage companies](https://www.saastr.com/openai-vs-anthropic-ramp-data-shows-36-vs-12-penetration-but-velocity-curves-tell-a-different-story/), not legacy enterprises). Large enterprises with existing Microsoft contracts may be undercounted in both datasets.

**Benchmark durability.** [LMSYS Arena rankings](https://arena.ai/leaderboard) shift with each new model release. Claude Opus 4.6 holds all three leaderboards now. GPT-5.5 is expected later in 2026. Benchmark leadership is a snapshot, not a permanent state.

**IPO market conditions.** Both companies assume the IPO window stays open through Q4 2026. A [market correction, regulatory action, or geopolitical event could close it](https://gizmodo.com/2026-is-poised-to-be-the-year-of-the-tech-ipo-will-it-also-be-the-year-the-ai-bubble-bursts-2000704395). And if both try to IPO in the same quarter, [investor capital may get split](https://fortune.com/2026/04/07/spacex-openai-anthropic-reopen-ipo-market-crunchbase/).

**Profitability timeline.** Anthropic's $30 billion revenue is impressive, but OpenAI [projects it won't be profitable until at least 2030](https://www.fastcompany.com/91522156/openai-doesnt-expect-to-be-profitable-until-at-least-2030-as-ai-costs-surge) despite $25 billion in annualized revenue. Anthropic's timeline is likely similar or worse given its higher growth-rate spending. Revenue leadership is not the same as business model sustainability.

## What This Means for Enterprise AI Strategy

For enterprises making AI platform decisions right now, the data suggests several practical considerations:

**Multi-vendor is the norm, not the exception.** [Most Anthropic customers also use OpenAI](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/11/openai-anthropic-chatgpt-claude-subscriptions). The question isn't which provider to choose — it's how to allocate workloads across providers based on task-specific performance and cost.

**The API layer is where the money moves.** Enterprise LLM spend is [overwhelmingly API-driven](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/). If your team is evaluating providers primarily through chatbot interfaces, you're optimizing for the wrong use case.

**Benchmark leadership correlates with enterprise adoption, but it's not the only factor.** Anthropic's [LMSYS Arena dominance](https://aiworld.eu/story/claude-opus-46-leader-of-the-leaderboards) coincides with its market share growth, but enterprises also cite safety practices, enterprise support quality, and [MCP ecosystem breadth](https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/anthropic-says-claude-code-transformed-programming-now-claude-cowork-is) as decision factors.

**Don't lock in based on current rankings.** Both companies will ship new models within months. The right architecture isolates your application logic from the model layer so you can switch providers without rewriting your codebase. MCP and [similar protocols](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/a2a-a-new-era-of-agent-interoperability/) exist precisely for this purpose.
