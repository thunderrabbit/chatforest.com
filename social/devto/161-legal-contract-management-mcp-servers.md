**At a glance:** 50+ servers bringing AI agents into law offices and contract workflows. The most geographically diverse MCP category — legal research servers cover **14+ countries**. E-signature servers cover 7 platforms but miss DocuSign and PandaDoc. No major legal tech vendor (Clio, LexisNexis, Westlaw, Ironclad) has released an official MCP server. **Rating: 3.5/5.**

## E-Signature & Document Signing (7 platforms)

- **SendForSign MCP** (official) — document templates and signature workflows
- **SignNow MCP** (official, airSlate, 28M+ users) — template management, embedded signing, document downloads
- **[caffeinebounce/docuseal-mcp-server](https://github.com/caffeinebounce/docuseal-mcp-server)** — DocuSeal (8K+ stars open-source platform), template and submission tracking
- **[bmbouter/mcp-dropbox-sign](https://github.com/bmbouter/mcp-dropbox-sign)** — Dropbox Sign API (formerly HelloSign)
- **[esignaturescom/mcp-server](https://github.com/esignaturescom/mcp-server)** — eSignatures.io, contract management
- **[signbee/signbee-mcp](https://github.com/signbee/signbee-mcp)** — markdown + PDF signing with certified delivery
- **e签宝 MCP** — Chinese e-signature platform with PDF conversion

Notable absence: **No DocuSign or PandaDoc** official MCP servers — the two largest platforms.

## Legal Research & Case Law (14+ Countries)

The standout subcategory — community developers worldwide have built MCP servers wrapping national legal databases.

**United States:**
- **[JamesANZ/us-legal-mcp](https://github.com/JamesANZ/us-legal-mcp)** — comprehensive U.S. legislation via government APIs
- **CourtListener MCP** — **3,352 U.S. courts**, federal and state
- **DocketBird MCP** — court document search and case detail retrieval

**Europe:**
- **[Mortalus/eu-regulations](https://github.com/Mortalus/eu-regulations)** — **37 EU regulations** including GDPR, AI Act, DORA, MiFID II
- **pylegifrance MCP** / **Droit Français MCP** — French legislation and case law
- **German Legal MCP** — federal/state/EU legislation
- **Danish-law-mcp** — Retsinformation portal
- **Swiss court decisions** + **legal commentaries** — two complementary servers

**Asia-Pacific:** South Korea (statutes/precedents), Japan (e-Gov Law API), Australia (2 servers: AusLaw + AustLII), Indonesia (data protection RAG)

**Latin America & Middle East:** Argentina (SAIJ + Buenos Aires courts), Brazil (Electronic Judicial Process), Turkey (Supreme/Constitutional Court + AI legal research), Poland (Sejm API)

## Legal Reasoning & Document Generation

- **[yoda-digital/mcp-cerebra-legal-server](https://github.com/yoda-digital/mcp-cerebra-legal-server)** — enterprise-grade legal reasoning with structured analysis frameworks
- **LegalForge MCP** — jurisdiction-specific document generation (privacy policies, terms of service)
- **[agentic-ops/legal-mcp](https://github.com/agentic-ops/legal-mcp)** — comprehensive legal workflow: research, case management, document analysis
- **Blawx MCP** — rule-based legal reasoning with formal ontologies (deterministic answers)

## IP & Trademarks

- **USPTO MCP** — Patent Trial and Appeal Board decisions with intelligent context reduction
- **Marka Patent MCP** — Turkish trademark database
- **Trademark Big Data MCP** — fuzzy search and portfolio analysis

## Compliance & Regulatory

- **HIPAA Guardian MCP** — healthcare compliance guidance for developers
- **FedRAMP Docs MCP** — FedRAMP requirements and security controls
- **Policy Analyzer MCP** — Google's IAM policy analysis
- **RegGuard MCP** — financial marketing compliance across jurisdictions
- **Aare MCP** — Z3 SMT solver formal verification of AI outputs against compliance ontologies

## What's Missing

No DocuSign, PandaDoc, Clio, LexisNexis, Westlaw, Ironclad, or Adobe Sign MCP servers. Limited contract lifecycle management. No legal billing/time tracking.

## Bottom Line

The geographic diversity is unmatched — 14+ countries with dedicated legal research servers. E-signatures cover 7 platforms. EU regulation access (37 acts via one server) is genuinely useful. But most servers are early-stage (0-20 stars), and the absence of major legal tech vendors leaves significant gaps.

**Rating: 3.5/5**
